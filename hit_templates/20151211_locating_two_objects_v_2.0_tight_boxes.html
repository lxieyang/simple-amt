Instructions

Given a picture, your task is to localize a person plus an object involved in the target activity. Suppose the target activity is riding a bicycle (as shown in the sample annotations below), your task is to draw two tight bounding boxes, one on the person (BLUE) and one on the bicycle he is riding (GREEN).

  1. First, find a person and an object involved in the target activity.
  2. Localize the person/object by drawing a TIGHT box around it.
    1. Start by choosing a proper location on the image to place the upper left vertex of your box.
    2. Left click your mouse and keep the left key down as your cursor moves around on the image. You can preview the resulting box as the cursor moves around.
    3. When you find the current box satisfying, complete the box by lifting your finger from the left key of your mouse.
  3. You will draw two tight boxes for each picture. FIRST for the person (BLUE). SECOND for the object the person is interacting with (GREEN).
  4. You will NOT be able to draw a box for the object before the box around the person is drawn.
  5. To undo mistakes, you can click the Reset button or hit R on your keyboard. This will ONLY reset the annotations you did to the CURRENT image. It does NOT affect your annotations to other images.
  6. If you see MULTIPLE people performing the target activity, please pick ONE instance and localize the associated person and object.
  7. Three sample annotations for riding a bicycle are shown below. If there are multiple people riding bicycles (the right picture), you can pick either one to annotate. Please make sure you annotate a person-object pair which involves interactions, for example, the left person along with the left bicycle.

Keyboard interface

  1. [→] / [←]  :  Go to the next/previous image
  2. R: Reset annotation.
